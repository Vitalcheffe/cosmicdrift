## 8. IEA Data on Morocco's Energy Mix

### Electricity Generation Mix (2024-2025)

| Source | Share (%) | Details | Source |
|--------|-----------|---------|--------|
| **Coal** | **~55%** | 30.86 TWh in 2024 (up from 30.14 TWh in 2023); 4.26 GW installed | Low-Carbon Power, Climatescope |
| **Wind** | **~21%** | 2,395 MW installed | Low-Carbon Power, IRENA |
| **Gas** | **~9%** | Combined cycle: 834 MW; Gas turbines: 1,011 MW | Low-Carbon Power, RES4Africa |
| **Hydro** | **~8–11%** | 1,306 MW installed | IRENA |
| **Solar** | **~7–8%** | 951 MW installed | IRENA, Low-Carbon Power |
| **Oil** | Small | Residual | IEA |

### Summary (2023-2024 data from multiple sources):
- **Fossil fuels: ~67–70%** (coal ~55%, gas ~9%, oil ~5%)
- **Renewables: ~30–33%** (wind ~20%, hydro ~8-11%, solar ~7-8%)
- Morocco imported **~90% of its energy needs** (hydrocarbons)
- Total electricity production: **~43 TWh** in 2023 (Energy Partnership Morocco-Germany)
- Low-carbon generation peaked in **2024**

### IEA Emissions Data (2023):
- Largest CO2 source by fuel: **Oil (57%)**
- Largest CO2 source by sector: **Electricity & heat (45%)**, Transport (28%)

## 4. Ghana's Grid Reliability Issues (Dumsor)

### Dumsor Timeline:
| Period | Event |
|--------|-------|
| **2006-2007** | Early power supply challenges |
| **2012-2016** | **Major Dumsor crisis** — severe power rationing, 12-24 hour blackouts; caused by low hydro generation (low water levels at Akosombo), gas supply disruptions from Nigeria via WAGP, and infrastructure failures |
| **2016** | Crisis officially declared over after Karpowership emergency barge deployed |
| **2017-2019** | Relative stability |
| **2020** | GRIDCo reported recurring outages |
| **2023-2025** | **Dumsor returns** — recurring outages blamed on unpaid utility bills (~$1.25B debt), poor infrastructure maintenance, gas supply constraints, and economic struggles; ECG debt crisis; 5 months of outages in 2025 |

### Key Statistics:
- Ghana has **almost 2x installed capacity vs peak demand** but still experiences outages due to financial/operational issues
- Causes: unpaid power sector debts, gas supply disruptions, equipment failures, transmission constraints
- Impact: Estimated 2-3% GDP loss during peak Dumsor years (2012-2016)

## 16. Ghana's Energy Efficiency Policies and Challenges

### Policies:
| Policy | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **MEPS (Minimum Energy Performance Standards)** | Introduced 2005 for lighting & air-conditioning; 2008 for refrigerating appliances |
| **Energy Efficiency Standards & Labelling (EES&L)** | Mandatory labeling for high-energy consuming appliances |
| **National Energy Efficiency Action Plan (NEEAP)** | Aligned with ECOWAS Energy Efficiency Policy (EEEP) |
| **Energy Commission Act 1997 (Act 541)** | Regulatory framework for electricity conservation |
| **MCC Compact** | Millennium Challenge Corporation funded energy efficiency programs in Ghana |
| **ECOFRIDGES Program** | United for Efficiency initiative for efficient refrigeration |
| **Refrigerator Exchange Program** | Replaced old inefficient refrigerators with efficient ones |
| **2024 Energy Efficiency Requirements** | New regulations for electrical appliances and renewable energy products (effective Nov 2024) |

### Challenges:
- **Enforcement gaps** — standards exist but enforcement is weak
- **Import of used/inefficient appliances** — significant grey market
- **Limited consumer awareness** — energy efficiency benefits not widely understood
- **Financial barriers** — upfront cost of efficient appliances is prohibitive for many
- **Building energy codes** — largely absent or unenforced
- **Industrial efficiency** — limited incentive programs for industrial consumers
- **Grid losses** — massive technical and commercial losses (27-32%) negate efficiency gains

## 17. Power Distribution Losses in Ghana

| Metric | Value | Year | Source |
|--------|-------|------|--------|
| **ECG system losses (cumulative)** | **27.05%** | 2024 | ECG Energy Accounting Report |
| **ECG losses (purchased power)** | **32%** | 2024 | Joy Business / Finex Insights |
| **Historical losses (1971-1999)** | **< 12%** | Historical | PURC Regulatory Brief |
| **Commercial + technical losses** | **~23%** | Recent | GhanaFact |
| **Best performing region** | Tema (8.60% losses) | 2024 | ECG |

**Critical finding:** ECG's losses of 27-32% are **far above international best practices** (typically 6-10% in developed countries). This represents an enormous waste — nearly 1/3 of all electricity purchased by ECG is lost through technical inefficiencies, theft, and commercial failures. This is the single biggest efficiency challenge in Ghana's power sector.

### Top 5 Hackathon-Relevant Findings:

1. **27-32% distribution losses** — the #1 efficiency challenge; nearly 1/3 of electricity is lost through technical inefficiencies, theft, and commercial failures
2. **Capacity paradox** — 5,260 MW installed vs 3,952 MW peak demand, yet outages persist due to financial/operational failures (not generation shortage)
3. **Dumsor recurrence** — chronic reliability issues (2012-2016 major crisis, 2023-2025 recurrence) undermining business and consumer confidence
4. **Low renewable penetration (~2%)** despite 10% by 2030 target — huge gap to close with only 5 years remaining
5. **Smart grid opportunity** — only early-stage deployment; 2.9M smart meter plan by 2029 represents massive IoT/smart grid opportunity for AI-driven efficiency solutions
